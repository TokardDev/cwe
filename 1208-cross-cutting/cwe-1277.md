## CWE Explanation

This weakness occurs when a device is designed without the capability to update or patch its firmware. In the lifecycle of hardware products, vulnerabilities are often discovered long after the device has been manufactured and deployed. If the firmware is immutable (e.g., burned into Read-Only Memory (ROM) or lacking a physical/logical update interface), it becomes impossible to fix these security flaws.

This "ship and forget" approach leaves users permanently vulnerable to known exploits. Even if the hardware itself is robust, software or firmware bugs are inevitable. Without an update mechanism, the only remediation is often the costly and impractical replacement of the physical hardware.

The root cause is usually a design trade-off for cost reduction (using cheaper ROM vs. Flash), a lack of long-term support planning, or security through obscurity assumptions.

## Impact Assessment

| Aspect | Impact |
|--------|--------|
| **Confidentiality** | The attacker can exploit unpatchable bugs to steal data |
| **Integrity** | The attacker can permanently compromise the device state |
| **Availability** | The attacker can disable the device, and no recovery/fix is possible |

## Attack Scenario

An attacker identifies a critical buffer overflow vulnerability in the network stack of a widely deployed smart refrigerator. The manufacturer acknowledges the vulnerability but cannot issue a fix because the device's firmware is stored on a Write-Once Read-Many (WORM) memory chip and the design includes no mechanism for external updates. The attacker proceeds to exploit this vulnerability across the internet, compromising thousands of these devices to form a botnet. The owners of the devices are unable to remediate the issue, and the devices remain permanently compromised until physically discarded.

## MITRE Reference Data

### Description

The product does not provide its users with the ability to update or patch its firmware to address any vulnerabilities or weaknesses that may be present.

### Extended Description

Without the ability to patch or update firmware, consumers will be left vulnerable to exploitation of any known vulnerabilities, or any vulnerabilities that are discovered in the future. This can expose consumers to permanent risk throughout the entire lifetime of the device, which could be years or decades. Some external protective measures and mitigations might be employed to aid in preventing or reducing the risk of malicious attack, but the root weakness cannot be corrected.
